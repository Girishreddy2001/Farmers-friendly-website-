Okra cultivation

  • Okra is a warm season crop. It is grown throughout the tropical and sub-tropical regions in the country.

  • Some of the most popular and profitable Indian okra varieties are Kamini, Pusa Mukhamali, Parbhani Kranti, etc.

  • Okra can be grown on a wide range of soils, having good internal drainage. Soils with high organic matter are preferred

  • For seed germination, optimum soil moisture and a temperature between 25°C and 35°C is needed ,Okra requires adequate moisture in the soil during summer months for faster growth.

  • Drip irrigation is most suitable to the crop as it provides uniform moisture throughout the season.

Redgram cultivation

  • Red gram is predominantly a crop of tropical areas mainly cultivated in semi-arid regions of india.

  • Red gram can be grown with a temperature ranging from 26 c to 30 c in the rainy season and 17 c to 22 c in the post rainly season.

  • It is succesfully grown in black cotton soils,well drained with a pH level ranging from 7.0 to 8.5.

  • Red gram responds well to properly tilled and then drained seedbed.light irrigation is required after sowing to ensure good seed germination.

  • One irrigation at flowering and subsequent irrigation after flowering are necessary depending upon the soil conditons and weather conditons.
